Legal Fees vs. Disbursements: Explained

When buying a home in Ontario, your final bill from a real estate lawyer consists of two main categories: Legal Fees and Disbursements.

At Cheap Home Closings, our Legal Fee is a flat $999. This pays for the lawyer's time, expertise, and document preparation.

Disbursements are out-of-pocket expenses the law firm pays to third parties on your behalf to complete the transaction. Instead of nickel-and-diming you with variable admin costs, we bundle our internal disbursements into a predictable $450 flat rate.

Included in $450 Internal Disbursements

  • Execution of Title & Writs searches
  • Courier and mailing fees
  • Bank wire transfer fees
  • Administrative and processing overhead

Excluded (Third-Party / Government Costs)

  • Land Transfer Tax (Ontario & Municipal)
  • Title Insurance Premium (Stewart Title, FCT, etc.)
  • Government Registration Fees (e.g., Transfer/Mortgage deeds - approx $84)
  • Third-party ID verification software fees (if required)

Ultimate Ontario Real Estate Law FAQ

Everything you need to know about closing costs, legal fees, and property transfers in Ontario.

Most real estate lawyers in Ontario charge between $1,200 to $2,500+ for a standard home purchase, often plus unpredictable disbursements. At Cheap Home Closings, we offer a flat $999 legal fee, plus a fixed $450 internal disbursement fee. This clear structure prevents surprise costs on closing day.

The Ontario Land Transfer Tax (LTT) is calculated on a sliding scale based on your purchase price:

  • 0.5% on the first $55,000
  • 1.0% on the amount between $55,000.01 and $250,000
  • 1.5% on the amount between $250,000.01 and $400,000
  • 2.0% on the amount between $400,000.01 and $2,000,000
  • 2.5% on the amount over $2,000,000

First-time homebuyers in Ontario may be eligible for a rebate of up to $4,000.

You only pay the Municipal Land Transfer Tax (MLTT) if the property you are purchasing is located within the geographic boundaries of the City of Toronto. This tax is applied in addition to the Provincial Land Transfer Tax. If you are buying in Mississauga, Markham, or Vaughan, you do not pay the Toronto MLTT. First-time buyers in Toronto can claim an additional MLTT rebate up to $4,475.

Title insurance protects you and your mortgage lender from losses related to the property's title or ownership. It covers issues like fraud, forgery, encroachments, zoning violations, and unknown liens. In Ontario, nearly all mortgage lenders require title insurance to fund a loan. It is a one-time premium paid on closing day based on the purchase price.
